Ludwig van Beethoven

Life and career

  • 1780-1792: Bonn - Beethoven began his studies with his most important teacher in Bonn, Christian Gottlob Neefe.
  • 1792-1802: Vienna - Beethoven left Bonn for Vienna in November 1792, amid rumours of war spilling out of France; he learned shortly after his arrival that his father had died.
  • 1802-1812: The 'heroic' period - Beethoven told the English pianist Charles Neate (in 1815) that he dated his hearing loss from a fit he suffered in 1798 induced by a quarrel with a singer.
  • 1813-1822: Acclaim - In early 1813 Beethoven apparently went through a difficult emotional period, and his compositional output dropped.
  • 1823-1827: The final years - The year 1823 saw the completion of three notable works, all of which had occupied Beethoven for some years, namely the Missa solemnis, the Ninth Symphony and the Diabelli Variations.
